High-Velocity 3D Printed Plane

This plane started as a stress test for high-speed printing and turned into a platform for playing with aerodynamics, structural optimization, and slicer tuning. It’s designed to survive launches, hard landings, and sustained loads up to ~15 g.

Build Overview

  • Modeled in Fusion 360 with separate control surfaces, spars, and modular nose sections.
  • Printed in foaming PLA for low-mass skins and TPU for impact-resistant nose and wingtips.
  • Carbon fiber spars embedded during printing to stiffen the wings and fuselage spine.

What I Focused On

  • Reducing print time by ~40% using custom profiles, gyroid infill, and aggressive accelerations.
  • Balancing stiffness vs. weight by iterating wall thickness, rib spacing, and spar placement.
  • Designing around real hardware: battery bay, servo pockets, wire channels, and CG tuning options.
