3D Printed Luminous Cube

A printed light cube that mixes hard edges with soft light. Designed as a desk-sized sculpture to explore light diffusion, panel thickness, and how 3D-printed geometry can control glow instead of just surviving it.

Role Concept, CAD, print, assembly
Tools Fusion 360 · Voron 2.4 · custom LED driver
Focus Light diffusion · panel geometry

Overview

The cube is a small lighting object built around the idea that you can “tune” glow with geometry and print settings. Each face uses slightly different wall thickness, infill, and patterning, so light leaks feel deliberate instead of random.

Design approach

  • Printed shell with selective thickening where I wanted sharper edges
  • Internal LED core mounted so it doesn’t hotspot a single panel
  • Tested multiple infill patterns to see how they scattered light

What I learned

This project was a great playground for understanding how printers handle thin walls and how much detail you can get away with on a small form factor. It also highlighted how “tiny” tweaks in infill and wall count can totally change how light wraps around a volume.

Stack
